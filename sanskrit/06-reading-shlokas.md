# Reading Shlokas — Lesson 6

## You Can Read Sanskrit Now

This is not an exaggeration. You know the sounds (varnamala), how they merge (sandhi), how nouns declare their roles (vibhakti), how verbs encode tense and person (lakaras), and how numbers and pronouns work. That is everything you need to decode real Sanskrit verse.

This lesson is where it all clicks. We take actual shlokas — lines that have been chanted for thousands of years — and take them apart, bolt by bolt.

---

## What is a Shloka?

**श्लोक** (śloka) — from the dhatu **श्लोक्** (ślok, to compose verse)

The standard shloka uses the **अनुष्टुभ् छन्दस्** (anuṣṭubh chandas) meter:

| Property | Value |
|---|---|
| Padas (quarters) | 4 |
| Syllables per pada | 8 |
| Total syllables | 32 |
| Structure | 2 lines, each with 2 padas |

```
Line 1:  ○ ○ ○ ○ ○ ○ ○ ○ | ○ ○ ○ ○ ○ ○ ○ ○
         pada 1 (8)          pada 2 (8)

Line 2:  ○ ○ ○ ○ ○ ○ ○ ○ | ○ ○ ○ ○ ○ ○ ○ ○
         pada 3 (8)          pada 4 (8)
```

This is by far the most common meter in Sanskrit literature. The Mahabharata (~100,000 shlokas), most of the Bhagavad Gita, the Ramayana, Subhashitas — all primarily anushtubh.

> Why 32 syllables? The anushtubh sits in the sweet spot — long enough for a complete thought, short enough to memorize. It is the iambic pentameter of Sanskrit.

---

## A Brief Note on Chandas (Meter)

**छन्दस्** (chandas) = meter, from **छद्** (chad, to cover/please) — "that which covers speech with rhythm"

Sanskrit verse isn't just counted by syllables. Each syllable is classified:

| Type | Sanskrit | Symbol | Rule |
|---|---|---|---|
| Light (laghu) | **लघु** | ◡ | Short vowel not followed by conjunct consonant |
| Heavy (guru) | **गुरु** | — | Long vowel, or short vowel followed by 2+ consonants |

In anushtubh, the key constraint is in **padas 2 and 4** (the even quarters):

- 5th syllable must be **laghu** (◡)
- 6th syllable must be **guru** (—)
- 7th syllable in pada 2: **guru** (—)
- 7th syllable in pada 4: **laghu** (◡)

This is enough to get started. Chandas is its own deep subject — Pingala's Chandahshastra catalogs dozens of meters — but recognizing anushtubh gets you through 80% of what you will read.

---

## The Reading Method

Every time you approach a shloka, follow this sequence:

```
1. Read aloud — let the sound register first
2. Split sandhi — break merged words back apart
3. Identify vibhakti — tag each noun's case
4. Find the verb — this is the anchor of the sentence
5. Reconstruct — who did what, to whom, where, how?
```

Sanskrit word order is free (vibhakti handles grammar), so poets arrange words for sound and rhythm, not for parsing convenience. You have to actively reconstruct the prose order. This is the skill.

## Shloka 1 — Opening of the Bhagavad Gita

> **धर्मक्षेत्रे कुरुक्षेत्रे समवेता युयुत्सवः ।**
> **मामकाः पाण्डवाश्चैव किमकुर्वत सञ्जय ॥**

*dharmakṣetre kurukṣetre samavetā yuyutsavaḥ |*
*māmakāḥ pāṇḍavāścaiva kimakurvata sañjaya ||*

This is Bhagavad Gita 1.1 — King Dhritarashtra's opening question to Sanjaya.

### Step 1: Sandhi Split

| Written | Split | Sandhi rule |
|---|---|---|
| पाण्डवाश्चैव | पाण्डवाः + च + एव | visarga sandhi (ाः → ाश् before च); च + एव → चैव (vṛddhi sandhi) |

The rest of this verse has minimal sandhi — most words stand cleanly.

### Step 2: Word-by-Word

| Word | Devanagari | Root / Form | Case | Meaning |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| dharmakṣetre | धर्मक्षेत्रे | dharma + kṣetra (field) | Saptami (locative, singular) | in the field of dharma |
| kurukṣetre | कुरुक्षेत्रे | kuru + kṣetra | Saptami (locative, singular) | in the field of the Kurus |
| samavetāḥ | समवेताः | sam + ava + √इ (i, to go) → samaveta (assembled) | Prathama (nominative, plural) | [those who] assembled |
| yuyutsavaḥ | युयुत्सवः | √युध् (yudh, to fight) → yuyutsu (desiring to fight, desiderative) | Prathama (nominative, plural) | desiring to fight |
| māmakāḥ | मामकाः | mama (my) + -ka (suffix) → māmaka (mine, my people) | Prathama (nominative, plural) | my [sons] |
| pāṇḍavāḥ | पाण्डवाः | pāṇḍu + -va (patronymic) → sons of Pandu | Prathama (nominative, plural) | the Pandavas |
| ca | च | conjunction | — | and |
| eva | एव | particle | — | indeed / also |
| kim | किम् | interrogative pronoun | — | what? |
| akurvata | अकुर्वत | √कृ (kṛ, to do), laṅ-lakāra (past tense), ātmanepada, prathama puruṣa, bahuvacana | — | did they do? |
| sañjaya | सञ्जय | proper name | Sambodhana (vocative) | O Sanjaya! |

### Step 3: Reconstruct

> **In the field of dharma, in the field of the Kurus, assembled and desiring to fight — my sons and the Pandavas — what did they do, O Sanjaya?**

Note the structure: two locatives set the scene, then nominatives identify the actors, then the verb-question arrives at the end. Dhritarashtra is blind — he cannot see the battlefield. This is the first line of 700 verses.

## Shloka 2 — Isha Upanishad (Shanti Mantra)

> **पूर्णमदः पूर्णमिदं पूर्णात् पूर्णमुदच्यते ।**
> **पूर्णस्य पूर्णमादाय पूर्णमेवावशिष्यते ॥**

*pūrṇamadaḥ pūrṇamidaṃ pūrṇāt pūrṇamudacyate |*
*pūrṇasya pūrṇamādāya pūrṇamevāvaśiṣyate ||*

### Step 1: Sandhi Split

| Written | Split | Rule |
|---|---|---|
| पूर्णमदः | पूर्णम् + अदः | m + a → ma (simple joining) |
| पूर्णमिदम् | पूर्णम् + इदम् | m + i → mi |
| पूर्णमुदच्यते | पूर्णम् + उदच्यते | m + u → mu |
| पूर्णमादाय | पूर्णम् + आदाय | m + ā → mā |
| पूर्णमेवावशिष्यते | पूर्णम् + एव + अवशिष्यते | m + e → me; a + a → ā (dīrgha sandhi) |

### Step 2: Word-by-Word

| Word | Root / Form | Case | Meaning |
|---|---|---|---|
| पूर्णम् (pūrṇam) | √पॄ (pṝ, to fill) → pūrṇa (full, complete) | Prathama (nom, neuter singular) | that which is full/complete |
| अदः (adaḥ) | demonstrative pronoun (that,远 — the distant one) | Prathama (nom, neuter singular) | that (invisible, beyond) |
| इदम् (idam) | demonstrative pronoun (this, 近 — the near one) | Prathama (nom, neuter singular) | this (visible, here) |
| पूर्णात् (pūrṇāt) | pūrṇa | Panchami (ablative, singular) | from the full |
| उदच्यते (udacyate) | उद् (ud, upward) + √चि (ci, to gather) → passive: "is produced/arises" | Verb: laṭ, karmavācya, prathama puruṣa, eka. | arises, is produced |
| पूर्णस्य (pūrṇasya) | pūrṇa | Shashthi (genitive, singular) | of the full |
| आदाय (ādāya) | आ + √दा (dā, to give) → ādā (to take away), lyap-kṛdanta (absolutive) | — | having taken away |
| अवशिष्यते (avaśiṣyate) | अव + √शिष् (śiṣ, to remain) → passive: "remains" | Verb: laṭ, karmavācya, prathama puruṣa, eka. | remains |
| एव (eva) | particle | — | only, indeed |

### Step 3: Reconstruct

> **That is full. This is full. From the full, the full arises.**
> **Having taken the full from the full, the full alone remains.**

One word — पूर्ण — used 6 times. The verse is a meditation on infinity: if you subtract infinity from infinity, infinity remains. This is the invocation of the Isha Upanishad, recited before the teaching begins.

## Shloka 3 — The Gayatri Mantra

> **ॐ भूर्भुवः स्वः**
> **तत्सवितुर्वरेण्यं भर्गो देवस्य धीमहि ।**
> **धियो यो नः प्रचोदयात् ॥**

*oṃ bhūr bhuvaḥ svaḥ*
*tat savitur vareṇyaṃ bhargo devasya dhīmahi |*
*dhiyo yo naḥ pracodayāt ||*

The Gayatri is from Rigveda 3.62.10 (attributed to Rishi Vishvamitra). It uses the **gāyatrī chandas** — 3 padas of 8 syllables each (24 total), distinct from the standard anushtubh.

### The Vyahrtis (Cosmic Utterances)

**ॐ भूर्भुवः स्वः** (oṃ bhūr bhuvaḥ svaḥ) — these are not part of the shloka proper. They are the three **व्याहृतियः** (vyāhṛtayaḥ), prefixed as an invocation:

| Vyahriti | Root | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| भूः (bhūḥ) | √भू (bhū, to be) | Earth / physical plane |
| भुवः (bhuvaḥ) | √भू (bhū) | Atmosphere / intermediate plane |
| स्वः (svaḥ) | √सु (su, well/good) or sva (self) | Heaven / celestial plane |

### Step 1: Sandhi Split

| Written | Split | Rule |
|---|---|---|
| तत्सवितुर्वरेण्यम् | तत् + सवितुः + वरेण्यम् | t + s → ts; visarga before v → r (ruki) |
| भर्गो | भर्गः + [next vowel triggers visarga → o] | visarga sandhi |
| धियो | धियः + [यो] | visarga sandhi (aḥ before voiced → o) |

### Step 2: Word-by-Word

| Word | Root / Form | Case | Meaning |
|---|---|---|---|
| तत् (tat) | demonstrative pronoun | Dvitiya (accusative, neuter singular) | that |
| सवितुः (savituḥ) | √सू (sū, to impel/generate) → सवितृ (savitṛ, the impeller — the Sun) | Shashthi (genitive, singular) | of Savitr (the sun as creative force) |
| वरेण्यम् (vareṇyam) | √वृ (vṛ, to choose) → vareṇya (worthy of choosing, most excellent) | Dvitiya (acc, neuter singular) | most excellent, worthy of worship |
| भर्गः (bhargaḥ) | √भ्रज् (bhraj, to shine) → bhargas (radiance, splendour) | Dvitiya (acc, neuter singular) | radiance, effulgence |
| देवस्य (devasya) | √दिव् (div, to shine) → deva (the shining one, deity) | Shashthi (genitive, singular) | of the deity |
| धीमहि (dhīmahi) | √धी (dhī, to meditate/contemplate), āśīrliṅ-lakāra / vidhiliṅ, first person plural | Verb | may we meditate upon |
| धियः (dhiyaḥ) | √धी (dhī) → dhī (intellect, thought) | Dvitiya (acc, plural) | [our] intellects / thoughts |
| यः (yaḥ) | relative pronoun | Prathama (nom, masculine singular) | who |
| नः (naḥ) | अस्मद् (asmad, we/us), enclitic form | Shashthi (genitive, plural) | our |
| प्रचोदयात् (pracodayāt) | प्र + √चुद् (cud, to impel) → causal form pracoday (to inspire/stimulate), āśīrliṅ / vidhiliṅ | Verb | may [he] inspire/impel |

### Step 3: Reconstruct

The natural prose order:

> **(यः) सवितुः देवस्य तत् वरेण्यं भर्गः धीमहि, (सः) नः धियः प्रचोदयात्**

> **May we meditate upon that most excellent radiance of the divine Savitr; may he inspire our intellects.**

Two verbs, two requests: (1) may we meditate on the light; (2) may that light sharpen our minds. That is all it says. No mysticism added — just a request for clarity.

## Shloka 4 — A Subhashita (Wise Saying)

> **विद्या ददाति विनयं विनयाद्याति पात्रताम् ।**
> **पात्रत्वाद्धनमाप्नोति धनाद्धर्मं ततः सुखम् ॥**

*vidyā dadāti vinayaṃ vinayādyāti pātratām |*
*pātratvāddhanamāpnoti dhanāddharmaṃ tataḥ sukham ||*

A famous subhashita on the chain of cause and effect, often attributed to the Hitopadesa.

### Step 1: Sandhi Split

| Written | Split | Rule |
|---|---|---|
| विनयाद्याति | विनयात् + याति | t + y → dy (voice assimilation) |
| पात्रत्वाद्धनम् | पात्रत्वात् + धनम् | t + dh → ddh (voice assimilation) |
| धनाद्धर्मम् | धनात् + धर्मम् | t + dh → ddh |
| आप्नोति | आ + √आप् (āp, to obtain) + नोति | no sandhi — this is morphological |

### Step 2: Word-by-Word

| Word | Root / Form | Case | Meaning |
|---|---|---|---|
| विद्या (vidyā) | √विद् (vid, to know) → vidyā (knowledge) | Prathama (nom, fem. singular) | knowledge |
| ददाति (dadāti) | √दा (dā, to give), laṭ, parasmaipada, 3rd singular | Verb | gives |
| विनयम् (vinayam) | वि + √नी (nī, to lead) → vinaya (humility, discipline) | Dvitiya (acc, singular) | humility |
| विनयात् (vinayāt) | vinaya | Panchami (ablative, singular) | from humility |
| याति (yāti) | √या (yā, to go/attain), laṭ, 3rd singular | Verb | one attains |
| पात्रताम् (pātratām) | pātra (vessel/worthy recipient) + -tā (abstract suffix: "-ness") | Dvitiya (acc, singular) | worthiness |
| पात्रत्वात् (pātratvāt) | pātra + -tva (abstract suffix, same as -tā) | Panchami (ablative, singular) | from worthiness |
| धनम् (dhanam) | √धा (dhā, to hold/place) → dhana (wealth) | Dvitiya (acc, singular) | wealth |
| आप्नोति (āpnoti) | आ + √आप् (āp, to obtain), laṭ, 3rd singular | Verb | one obtains |
| धनात् (dhanāt) | dhana | Panchami (ablative, singular) | from wealth |
| धर्मम् (dharmam) | √धृ (dhṛ, to hold/sustain) → dharma | Dvitiya (acc, singular) | dharma (right action) |
| ततः (tataḥ) | adverb | — | from that, then |
| सुखम् (sukham) | su (good) + kha (space/axle-hole) → sukha (ease, happiness) | Dvitiya (acc, singular) | happiness |

### Step 3: Reconstruct

> **Knowledge gives humility. From humility, one attains worthiness.**
> **From worthiness, one obtains wealth. From wealth, dharma. From dharma, happiness.**

The chain: विद्या → विनय → पात्रता → धन → धर्म → सुख. Five ablatives (from X comes Y) build a causal cascade. Notice every pada has its own subject-verb-object, and the ablative is the link between them.

## Shloka 5 — Saraswati Vandana

> **या कुन्देन्दुतुषारहारधवला या शुभ्रवस्त्रावृता**
> **या वीणावरदण्डमण्डितकरा या श्वेतपद्मासना ।**
> **या ब्रह्माच्युतशङ्करप्रभृतिभिर्देवैः सदा वन्दिता**
> **सा मां पातु सरस्वती भगवती निःशेषजाड्यापहा ॥**

*yā kundendutuṣārahāradhavalā yā śubhravastrāvṛtā*
*yā vīṇāvaradaṇḍamaṇḍitakarā yā śvetapadmāsanā |*
*yā brahmācyutaśaṅkaraprabhṛtibhirdevaiḥ sadā vanditā*
*sā māṃ pātu sarasvatī bhagavatī niḥśeṣajāḍyāpahā ||*

This verse is not anushtubh — it uses the **शार्दूलविक्रीडित** (śārdūlavikrīḍita, "tiger's sport") meter, 19 syllables per pada. A longer, more ornate meter for formal invocations.

### Step 1: Key Sandhi Splits

| Written | Split | Rule |
|---|---|---|
| कुन्देन्दुतुषारहारधवला | कुन्द + इन्दु + तुषार + हार + धवला | compound (no sandhi — this is a samāsa, a compound word) |
| शुभ्रवस्त्रावृता | शुभ्र + वस्त्र + आवृता | compound |
| वीणावरदण्डमण्डितकरा | वीणा + वर + दण्ड + मण्डित + करा | compound |
| ब्रह्माच्युतशङ्करप्रभृतिभिः | ब्रह्मा + अच्युत + शङ्कर + प्रभृति + भिः | compound + instrumental plural ending |
| निःशेषजाड्यापहा | निः + शेष + जाड्य + अपहा | niḥ (without) + śeṣa (remainder) + jāḍya (dullness) + apahā (remover) |

### Step 2: Word-by-Word (Key Words)

| Word | Root / Compound | Case | Meaning |
|---|---|---|---|
| या (yā) | relative pronoun (feminine) | Prathama (nom, fem. sing.) | she who |
| कुन्देन्दुतुषारहारधवला | kunda (jasmine) + indu (moon) + tuṣāra (frost) + hāra (garland) + dhavalā (white) | Prathama (nom) | white as jasmine, moon, frost, and pearl-garlands |
| शुभ्रवस्त्रावृता | śubhra (bright) + vastra (garment) + āvṛtā (covered) | Prathama (nom) | draped in bright garments |
| वीणावरदण्डमण्डितकरा | vīṇā + vara (excellent) + daṇḍa (stem/neck) + maṇḍita (adorned) + karā (hand) | Prathama (nom) | whose hand is adorned with the fine neck of a veena |
| श्वेतपद्मासना | śveta (white) + padma (lotus) + āsanā (seated) | Prathama (nom) | seated on a white lotus |
| प्रभृतिभिः (prabhṛtibhiḥ) | pra + √bhṛ (bhṛ, to bear) → prabhṛti (beginning with, et cetera) | Tritiya (instrumental) | beginning with, led by |
| देवैः (devaiḥ) | deva | Tritiya (instrumental, plural) | by the gods |
| सदा (sadā) | adverb | — | always |
| वन्दिता (vanditā) | √वन्द् (vand, to praise/bow to), past passive participle | Prathama (nom) | praised, worshipped |
| सा (sā) | demonstrative pronoun (fem.) | Prathama (nom) | she |
| माम् (mām) | अस्मद् (I/me) | Dvitiya (acc) | me |
| पातु (pātu) | √पा (pā, to protect), loṭ-lakāra (imperative), 3rd singular | Verb | may she protect |
| सरस्वती (sarasvatī) | saras (flow/lake) + -vatī (possessing) | Prathama (nom) | Saraswati — "she who possesses flow" |
| भगवती (bhagavatī) | bhaga (glory/fortune) + -vat + feminine -ī | Prathama (nom) | the divine one |
| निःशेषजाड्यापहा | complete remover of all dullness (see split above) | Prathama (nom) | she who removes all traces of stupidity |

### Step 3: Reconstruct

> **She who is white as jasmine, moon, frost, and pearl-garlands; she who is draped in bright garments; she whose hand is adorned with the fine neck of a veena; she who is seated on a white lotus; she who is always worshipped by Brahma, Vishnu, Shiva, and the other gods — may that Saraswati, that divine one, the complete remover of all dullness, protect me.**

The entire shloka is one sentence. Four **या** (yā, "she who...") clauses build suspense, stacking descriptions. Then **सा** (sā, "she") resolves the sentence with a single verb: **पातु** (may she protect). This is the relative-correlative construction — या...सा — one of Sanskrit's most characteristic sentence patterns.

## Shloka 6 — Asato Ma (Brihadaranyaka Upanishad 1.3.28)

> **असतो मा सद्गमय ।**
> **तमसो मा ज्योतिर्गमय ।**
> **मृत्योर्मा अमृतं गमय ॥**

*asato mā sadgamaya |*
*tamaso mā jyotirgamaya |*
*mṛtyormā amṛtaṃ gamaya ||*

### Step 1: Sandhi Split

| Written | Split | Rule |
|---|---|---|
| असतो | असतः | visarga sandhi (aḥ → o before voiced) |
| सद्गमय | सत् + गमय | t + g → dg (voice assimilation) |
| तमसो | तमसः | same visarga sandhi |
| ज्योतिर्गमय | ज्योतिः + गमय | visarga → r before voiced consonant |
| मृत्योर्मा | मृत्योः + मा | visarga → r before m |

### Step 2: Word-by-Word

| Word | Root / Form | Case | Meaning |
|---|---|---|---|
| असतः (asataḥ) | a (not) + √अस् (as, to be) → asat (unreal, non-being) | Panchami (ablative, singular) | from the unreal |
| मा (mā) | अस्मद् (me), enclitic accusative | Dvitiya (acc) | me |
| सत् (sat) | √अस् (as, to be) → sat (real, being, truth) | — (adverbial/accusative goal) | to the real |
| गमय (gamaya) | √गम् (gam, to go), causal (णिच्) imperative, 2nd person singular | Verb | lead! (cause to go) |
| तमसः (tamasaḥ) | √तम् (tam, to be exhausted/dark) → tamas (darkness) | Panchami (ablative) | from darkness |
| ज्योतिः (jyotiḥ) | √ज्युत् (jyut, to shine) → jyotis (light) | — (goal) | to light |
| मृत्योः (mṛtyoḥ) | √मृ (mṛ, to die) → mṛtyu (death) | Panchami (ablative) | from death |
| अमृतम् (amṛtam) | a (not) + mṛta (dead) → amṛta (deathless, immortal) | — (goal) | to immortality |

### Step 3: Reconstruct

> **From the unreal, lead me to the real.**
> **From darkness, lead me to light.**
> **From death, lead me to immortality.**

Three lines, one structure: **ablative + मा + goal + गमय**. The verb is causative — not "let me go" but "make me go." This is not a passive wish. It is a direct instruction to the teacher (or the divine): move me from here to there.

## Answers

### Practice 1

**Sandhi splits:**
- सिध्यन्ति ← no split needed (√सिध् + यन्ति)
- कार्याणि ← no split needed (कार्य + plural neuter nom.)
- मनोरथैः ← मनः + रथैः (visarga sandhi: aḥ + r → o)
- सुप्तस्य ← no split (√स्वप् → supta, past participle + genitive)
- प्रविशन्ति ← प्र + √विश् + अन्ति

**Word-by-word:**

| Word | Root | Case | Meaning |
|---|---|---|---|
| उद्यमेन (udyamena) | उद् + √यम् (yam, to restrain/exert) → udyama (effort) | Tritiya (instrumental) | by effort |
| हि (hi) | particle | — | indeed |
| सिध्यन्ति (sidhyanti) | √सिध् (sidh, to succeed), laṭ, 3rd plural | Verb | succeed, are accomplished |
| कार्याणि (kāryāṇi) | √कृ (kṛ, to do) → kārya (that which is to be done, task) | Prathama (nom, neuter plural) | tasks |
| न (na) | — | — | not |
| मनोरथैः (manorathaiḥ) | manas (mind) + ratha (chariot) → manoratha (desire, daydream — "mind-chariot") | Tritiya (instrumental, plural) | by daydreams |
| सुप्तस्य (suptasya) | √स्वप् (svap, to sleep) → supta (sleeping) | Shashthi (genitive, singular) | of a sleeping |
| सिंहस्य (siṃhasya) | siṃha (lion) | Shashthi (genitive, singular) | lion's |
| प्रविशन्ति (praviśanti) | प्र + √विश् (viś, to enter), laṭ, 3rd plural | Verb | enter |
| मुखे (mukhe) | mukha (mouth) | Saptami (locative, singular) | in the mouth |
| मृगाः (mṛgāḥ) | √मृग् (mṛg, to seek) → mṛga (deer, prey) | Prathama (nom, plural) | deer/prey |

**Translation:**

> **Tasks are accomplished by effort, not by daydreams. Deer do not enter the mouth of a sleeping lion.**

### Practice 2

**Sandhi splits:**
- तथैव ← तथा + एव (ā + e → ai, vṛddhi sandhi)
- धर्महिंसा ← this is a compound: dharma + hiṃsā (violence for dharma's sake)

**Word-by-word:**

| Word | Root | Case | Meaning |
|---|---|---|---|
| अहिंसा (ahiṃsā) | a (not) + √हिंस् (hiṃs, to harm) → ahiṃsā (non-violence) | Prathama (nom) | non-violence |
| परमः (paramaḥ) | parama (highest, supreme) | Prathama (nom) | the highest |
| धर्मः (dharmaḥ) | √धृ (dhṛ) → dharma | Prathama (nom) | dharma |
| धर्महिंसा (dharmahiṃsā) | dharma + hiṃsā (violence in service of dharma) | Prathama (nom) | righteous [use of] force |
| तथा (tathā) | adverb | — | likewise, so too |
| एव (eva) | particle | — | indeed |
| च (ca) | — | — | and |

**Translation:**

> **Non-violence is the highest dharma. And so too is righteous force.**

This is the full verse often cut short. The first half alone is famous. The second half complicates it — sometimes dharma requires force. The verse does not resolve the tension. It holds both.
