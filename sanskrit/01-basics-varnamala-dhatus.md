# Sanskrit Basics — Lesson 1

## Why Sanskrit?

Sanskrit = **sam** (together/complete) + **krita** (made) = "perfectly constructed"

You can't understand Hindu philosophy without understanding the language it thinks in. Sanskrit isn't just a vehicle for ideas — it IS the architecture.

- **Compositional**: words built from root sounds (dhatus) + prefixes + suffixes
- **Panini's Ashtadhyayi** (~500 BCE): ~4,000 generative grammar rules — the first formal language specification

---

## The Alphabet (Varnamala)

Sanskrit's alphabet is a **sound map** — organized by where in your mouth and how the sound is produced.

### Vowels (Svara — "that which shines by itself")

| Short | Long | Transliteration |
|---|---|---|
| अ | आ | a / ā |
| इ | ई | i / ī |
| उ | ऊ | u / ū |
| ऋ | ॠ | ṛ / ṝ |
| ए | ऐ | e / ai |
| ओ | औ | o / au |

**अ (a)** is the default sound — every consonant carries it unless told otherwise. क is not "k", it's "ka".

### AUM (अ + उ + म)

From the Mandukya Upanishad:
- **अ** = waking state (creation)
- **उ** = dream state (preservation)
- **म** = deep sleep (dissolution)
- The silence after = Turiya (pure consciousness)

### Consonants (Vyanjana — "that which manifests with help")

Organized by mouth position — throat to lips:

| Location | Unvoiced | Voiced | Nasal |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Throat** (Kanthya) | क ka, ख kha | ग ga, घ gha | ङ ṅa |
| **Palate** (Talavya) | च cha, छ chha | ज ja, झ jha | ञ ña |
| **Roof** (Murdhanya) | ट ṭa, ठ ṭha | ड ḍa, ढ ḍha | ण ṇa |
| **Teeth** (Dantya) | त ta, थ tha | द da, ध dha | न na |
| **Lips** (Oshthya) | प pa, फ pha | ब ba, भ bha | म ma |

Each row: throat → lips. Each column: less energy → more energy → nasal.

## The 5 Core Dhatus (Root Verbs)

| Dhatu | Meaning | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| **भू** (bhū) | to be, to become | bhūmi (earth), bhāva (state of being), bhūta (element) |
| **कृ** (kṛ) | to do, to make | karma (action), saṃskṛta (Sanskrit), kṛṣṇa (the dark one) |
| **गम** (gam) | to go | āgama (arrival/scripture), gati (movement/destiny) |
| **विद** (vid) | to know | veda (knowledge), vidyā (learning), avidyā (ignorance) |
| **वच** (vac) | to speak | vāk (speech), vachana (statement) |

**karma** = कृ (to do) + man (suffix) = "the thing that is done"

---

## Word Breakdowns

- **Ayurveda** = āyus (life) + veda (knowledge) → "knowledge of life"
- **Yoga** = yuj (to yoke/unite) → "union"
- **Nirvana** = nir (without) + vāna (blowing/desire) → "extinguishing"
- **Guru** = gu (darkness) + ru (remover) → "remover of darkness"
- **Mantra** = man (to think) + tra (instrument) → "instrument of thought"
