# Sandhi — The Rules of Sound Merging

## What is Sandhi?

**सन्धि** (sandhi) = **सम्** (sam, together) + **धि** (dhi, from धा/dhā, to place) → "placing together"

When two Sanskrit sounds meet — at the end of one word and the start of the next, or within a compound — they **transform**. They don't just sit side by side. They merge, shift, or replace each other according to precise rules.

This is not optional. This is how the language breathes.

> In English: "do not" → "don't" is informal, optional.
> In Sanskrit: sandhi is the grammar. The unmerged form is the exception.

---

## Why Sandhi Matters

1. **You can't read texts without it** — every shloka, every sutra has sandhi applied. If you don't know the rules, you can't split words apart to find their meaning.
2. **It reveals how the language thinks** — sounds aren't isolated units, they exist in relationship. Context changes identity.
3. **It's the original tokenizer** — just as an LLM merges subwords into tokens based on context and frequency, Sanskrit merges sounds based on phonetic rules. The boundary between words is fluid.

---

## The Three Types of Sandhi

| Type | Where it happens | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **स्वरसन्धि** (Svara Sandhi) | Vowel + Vowel | Two vowels meet at a word boundary |
| **व्यञ्जनसन्धि** (Vyanjana Sandhi) | Consonant + Consonant/Vowel | A consonant ending meets the next sound |
| **विसर्गसन्धि** (Visarga Sandhi) | Visarga (ः) + next sound | The breath-sound ः transforms |

---

## 1. Svara Sandhi (Vowel Sandhi)

The most common. When a vowel at the end of one word meets a vowel at the start of the next.

### Rule 1: Sama (Same) — Like merges with like

When two similar vowels meet, they combine into the long version.

```
अ + अ = आ       a + a = ā
इ + इ = ई       i + i = ī
उ + उ = ऊ       u + u = ū
```

**Example:**
- **देव + अलयः** = **देवालयः**
- deva + ālayaḥ = devālayaḥ
- "god" + "abode" = "temple"

The अ at the end of देव and the आ at the start of आलय merge into आ.

### Rule 2: Guna — Short upgrades to medium

When अ/आ meets इ/ई, उ/ऊ, or ऋ/ॠ, they combine into a "guna" vowel.

```
अ/आ + इ/ई = ए       a + i = e
अ/आ + उ/ऊ = ओ       a + u = o
अ/आ + ऋ/ॠ = अर्     a + ṛ = ar
```

**Example:**
- **महा + ईश्वरः** = **महेश्वरः**
- mahā + īśvaraḥ = maheśvaraḥ
- "great" + "lord" = "Maheshvara" (name of Shiva)

The आ + ई merge into ए.

### Rule 3: Vriddhi — Short upgrades to strongest

When अ/आ meets ए/ऐ or ओ/औ, they intensify.

```
अ/आ + ए/ऐ = ऐ       a + e/ai = ai
अ/आ + ओ/औ = औ       a + o/au = au
```

**Example:**
- **एक + एक** = **एकैक**
- eka + eka = ekaika
- "one" + "one" = "each one"

### The Vowel Strength Ladder

```
Weak (Hrasva):    अ    इ    उ    ऋ
Medium (Guna):    अ    ए    ओ    अर्
Strong (Vriddhi): आ    ऐ    औ    आर्
```

This isn't random — it's an **energy gradient**. Sounds move UP the ladder when they merge, never down.

## 2. Vyanjana Sandhi (Consonant Sandhi)

When a word ends in a consonant and the next word begins.

### Key Rule: Voice matches voice

A final unvoiced consonant becomes voiced before a voiced sound.

```
वाक् + ईशः = वागीशः
vāk + īśaḥ = vāgīśaḥ
"speech" + "lord" = "lord of speech" (name of Brahma/Saraswati)
```

The क् (k, unvoiced) becomes ग् (g, voiced) because ई is voiced.

### Key Rule: Nasal assimilation

A nasal takes the position of the consonant that follows it.

```
Before क/ख/ग/घ → ङ (throat nasal)
Before च/छ/ज/झ → ञ (palate nasal)
Before ट/ठ/ड/ढ → ण (roof nasal)
Before त/थ/द/ध → न (teeth nasal)
Before प/फ/ब/भ → म (lip nasal)
```

This is why the consonant grid from Lesson 1 matters — sandhi follows the same map.

---

## 3. Visarga Sandhi

The **विसर्ग** (visarga, ः) is a soft breath at the end of a word. It's unstable — it transforms based on what follows.

```
Before voiced consonant/vowel: ः often → ओ or drops
Before क/ख: ः stays or becomes स्
Before च/छ: ः becomes श्
Before ट/ठ: ः becomes ष्
Before त/थ: ः becomes स्
```

**Example:**
- **रामः + च** = **रामश्च**
- rāmaḥ + ca = rāmaśca
- "Rama" + "and" = "and Rama"

---

## Sandhi in Action: Deconstructing a Famous Phrase

**सत्यमेव जयते** (satyameva jayate) — "Truth alone triumphs"

India's national motto. Let's reverse the sandhi:

```
सत्यम् + एव → सत्यमेव
satyam + eva → satyameva
```

The म् (m) before ए (e, a vowel) stays as म according to sandhi rules, and the words fuse.

- **सत्य** (satya) = truth — from root **सत्** (sat, being/existence)
- **एव** (eva) = alone, indeed
- **जयते** (jayate) = triumphs — from root **जि** (ji, to conquer)

> From the Mundaka Upanishad (3.1.6):
> सत्यमेव जयते नानृतम्
> "Truth alone triumphs, not untruth"

## Key Takeaway

Sandhi means **nothing exists in isolation**. Every sound is shaped by its neighbours. Identity is relational.

> This is प्रतीत्यसमुत्पाद (pratītyasamutpāda) — dependent origination — expressed at the level of phonetics.
